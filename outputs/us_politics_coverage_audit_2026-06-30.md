## TL;DR

Ripple's US-politics coverage is a deep, clean SPINE with almost no body. The bioguide-keyed 'box score' is genuinely strong: member ideology (DW-NOMINATE), the vote matrix, bill status + cosponsors, and FEC reference/summary money are all loaded and modeled into tidy POLITICS marts on a STEEL crosswalk (bioguide↔icpsr↔fec_cand_id). Adjacent accountability sources (FARA 222k, Federal Register, SCDB, OFAC, USASpending contracts) are also landed. That's the whole strength — and it's all federal, all incumbent-Congress, all current-Congress (118th-119th). Three biggest blind spots: (1) NO itemized money — you have every dimension table but not one transaction (Schedule A/B/pas2/oth/IE all unloaded), so you can't follow a dollar; the designated next build (FEC itcont) is correctly the top priority. (2) NO substance or structure around the box score — zero bill text, zero committee membership, zero lobbying (and the easy LDA source dies TODAY), zero nominations, zero floor speech. (3) NOTHING below federal-incumbent and NOTHING on outcomes — no state legislative data, no local officials, and no election RESULTS at any level (you track who raised money but not who won). Whole categories sit at zero: lobbying, scorecards, surveys, 527s/dark money, ads, grants/loans, lower judiciary, PTRs/financial disclosure, earmarks, communications. The fastest high-value wins are clean federal keyed sources that bolt onto the existing spine — pas2 (PAC→cand edges), committee membership, BILLSUM, Demand Progress earmarks (pre-keyed to bioguide), EveryCRSReport — followed by the urgent time-boxed LDA load and the first real outcome data (MEDSL results, FIPS+fec_cand_id-bridgeable). Two infra fixes also needed: the SAM exclusions load is broken (1k of 167k rows) and the FPDS→SAM API migration lands before summer 2026.

## Headline gaps

- You have ZERO bill TEXT. You have the box score (BILLSTATUS sponsors/cosponsors/actions, 36k bills) but not a single word of what any bill actually SAYS — GovInfo BILLS/BILLSUM/PLAW are all unloaded.
- You have ZERO federal lobbying. Senate LDA (LD-1/LD-2/LD-203) is registered-not-loaded, and lda.senate.gov DIES after today (2026-06-30) — migrate to LDA.gov now or lose the easy backfill window.
- You have ZERO committee membership. You can't answer 'who chairs what' — @unitedstates committee-membership YAML (current) + Stewart (historical, ICPSR-keyed) are both unloaded despite being trivial.
- You have ZERO itemized money. cn/ccl/weball/cm are loaded but Schedule A (itcont), Schedule B (oppexp), pas2 (PAC→cand), oth (cmte→cmte), and Schedule E (IEs) are all gaps. You have the dimensions, none of the transactions.
- You have ZERO state legislative anything. No state bills, votes, legislators, ideology, or money despite Open States + LegiScan being scouted and Shor-McCarty (state DW-NOMINATE) sitting free on Dataverse.
- You have ZERO election RESULTS at any level. You track who RAISED money but not who WON — MEDSL (federal, FIPS+fec_cand_id-bridgeable) and OpenElections are unloaded.
- You have ZERO ethics/PTR/financial-disclosure DATA. House PTR + OGE 278e are scouted-not-loaded; the House Clerk <YEAR>FD.xml index is a trivial official spine you don't have.
- You have ZERO judiciary below SCOTUS. SCDB+Oyez modeled, but no FJC judge directory, no CourtListener, no judicial financial disclosures, no Judicial Common Space (the bridge to your own DW-NOMINATE).
- You have ZERO political ad spend. Google Political Ads (clean BigQuery), Meta Ad Library, and FCC OPIF are all untouched.
- Your SAM exclusions load is BROKEN: 1,000 of ~167,000 rows landed — it silently can't power debarred_but_funded at scale.
- You have ZERO grants/loans/sub-awards. USASpending CONTRACTS only (~6.3M rows); the grants, loans, and FSRS sub-award legs are gaps in the same API you already call.
