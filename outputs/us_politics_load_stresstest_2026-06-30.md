## Verdict

The plans mostly survived the stress test, but NOT as written. The infrastructure (the chunked loader, density gate, append-only registry, budget guard, publish-safety human-review gate) is solid and verified in code. Where the plans crack is the SCIENCE of the money. Scope 1 (FEC itemized) is the dangerous one: every transaction-file correctness control the plan defers to staging (cross-file SUB_ID dedup, conduit/memo attribution, 24A-vs-24E split, quote-aware parsing, a transaction-level penny referee) does NOT exist yet, and the loader it reuses (build_money_spine.read_fec) is verified to split('|') with no quotechar, hold the whole 30-40GB file in RAM via zf.read(), and pad/truncate-and-warn instead of raising. So itcont as sequenced OOMs before spending a credit AND can silently ship a wrong follow-the-money figure (the cardinal sin). Scope 1 = go_with_fixes, but only after real work, not 'trivial.' Scope 2 (LDA) = go_with_fixes: clean infra path, but the windowing target (<10k) contradicts its own ~2500 page-100 ceiling, the 11hr backfill straddles the PAT cliff with no real checkpoint table, and 'lobbying $ per bill' is a libel-grade fuzzy mart. Scope 3 (SAM/USASpending) = go_with_fixes, bordering on reconsider for the SAM piece: the proposed SAM 'fix' is actually a REGRESSION — the existing v4 loader already carries UEI+activation+termination (verified sam_exclusions_load.py:70,84,85), and the active-only CSV would drop UEI and silently disarm the debarred_but_funded detector this load exists to feed. The USASpending assistance bug (reusing the contract COLUMNS list silently drops CFDA/FAIN/URI at usaspending_load.py:133) is the single most concrete loader bug found, and it is verified. Cross-cutting: the PAT 5-day cliff (expires 2026-07-05, today 06-30) is the dominant blocker — it kills any multi-hour load mid-stream and the resume logic is verified dup-unsafe under an auth-death. Budget is near-red from PRIOR spend (per-load cost is ~nothing — verified ~0.14cr for 40 loads), so the real gate is month-to-date headroom, not itcont's row count. Bottom line: rotate the PAT first, ship the cheap/safe loads now, and do NOT start itcont or the LDA full backfill until the correctness referees and resumable-chunking exist.
