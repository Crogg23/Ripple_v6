# Ripple -- The Frontier (what we could go get next)

*Ripple already sees the money, the votes, the judges, and the doctors. Here's the data that would let it see the whole board -- and watch power move across it.*

## The keystones

The plumbing that makes every other map honest. Get these and everything Ripple already holds gets richer overnight.

- **Get Census / ACS tract-level demographics** -> the population underneath every dot Ripple already plots. Turns "a lot of overdoses in County X" into "overdoses per capita, by race, in the tracts redlined in 1935." Without it, every map is just a population map.
- **Get the HUD ZIP<->Tract<->County<->District crosswalk** -> the universal adapter. Lands a donor's ZIP inside a congressional district so a member's votes can be lined up against the demographics of the money flowing in versus the people they represent.
- **Get Census TIGER/Line boundary shapes** -> the geometry to actually *draw* everything, including redrawn district lines each cycle -- so gerrymandering becomes a map, not a rumor.
- **Get BLS QCEW county-by-industry employment + wages** -> the quarterly economic baseline. The denominator that turns worker deaths, wage theft, and layoffs into rates, and catches an industry collapsing in near-real-time under the deaths-of-despair curve.

## Follow the bodies

- **Get OSHA enforcement** -> plot every killed and maimed worker on the map, sized by penalty, colored by whether the employer is a federal contractor -- and watch the same firms collect fatalities while collecting bigger awards and cutting bigger checks.
- **Get FDA FAERS adverse events** -> line each drug's rising tide of injury and death reports against the pharma money flowing to its top prescribers -- safety signal vs. sales effort, side by side, drug by drug.
- **Get DOJ Deaths in Custody + Vera's 50-year incarceration record** -> animate the carceral boom county by county since 1970 and overlay who dies inside -- watch mass incarceration bloom out of the same neighborhoods redlined in the 1930s.
- **Get EPA EJScreen (the de-hosted Harvard mirror)** -> lay the pre-joined pollution-x-demographics fabric over Ripple's overdose and police-killing layers to ask whether the "sacrifice zone" is one place or three separate ones.

## Public money, private profit

- **Get NIH RePORTER grants + Drugs@FDA approvals** -> follow a single molecule: the taxpayer science that funded it, the approval that gave it a monopoly, the launch price, the payments to doctors -- rank drugs by how much public money underwrote a blockbuster that then soaked Medicare.
- **Get CMS Hospital Price Transparency files** -> put the same hip replacement's negotiated price on a map, hospital by hospital, and watch it swing 5-10x across one town -- then check whether the priciest are the PE-backed ones sitting in the poorest ZIPs.
- **Get SBA PPP loan-level data** -> shade counties by pandemic dollars-per-capita against donation intensity and 2020 vote margins -- did political proximity predict who got bailed out?
- **Get SEC Form ADV private-fund filings** -> trace the private-equity house behind a nursing home, then plot its facilities' care quality against the quarter the buyout fund showed up. Does care collapse after the money arrives?

## Who really owns it

- **Get SEC Exhibit 21 subsidiary trees** -> watch a contractor or pharma parent's subsidiary tree metastasize year over year, sprouting a Cayman shell right before a big contract or an offshore profit-shift -- and finally roll a sanctioned entity up to its listed parent.
- **Get USASpending sub-awards + SAM.gov parent hierarchy** -> roll every prime contract up to its ultimate owner and down to its subcontractors, then watch a parent's total federal take rise in lockstep with its PAC money to the very committees that fund it.
- **Get county parcel + assessor records** -> color the map as each house flips from a person's name to an LLC, then run that ownership-flip against the eviction wave and the redlining lines. Does Wall Street buy hardest exactly where the government drew red?

## The paid-persuasion layer

- **Get federal lobbying disclosures (LDA)** -> watch a corporation's lobbying dollars ramp on a specific bill number in the quarters before a committee vote, then line it up against how the members its PAC also funded actually voted. The missing verb between the money and the vote.
- **Get congressional stock trades (STOCK Act)** -> overlay a member's personal buys and sells against the committees they sit on and the bills hitting that sector -- a health-subcommittee member buying pharma weeks before a favorable vote, in a company already in Ripple's Open Payments data.
- **Get state campaign finance + Open States legislatures** -> reconstruct the money-to-vote loop Ripple only has federally, and catch the same near-identical model bill surfacing in a dozen statehouses the same year one industry's donations rise.
- **Get IRS 990 dark-money grant schedules** -> chain the grants flowing org-to-org between "social welfare" groups to reveal the hidden plumbing lighting up in the two years before an election.

## The 90-year map

- **Get HMDA mortgage records** -> lay 2007-2025 loan denial rates by race directly onto the 1930s HOLC polygons -- does the red ink of 1935 still predict who gets told "no" today? Then join the lenders to the House Financial Services members they fund.
- **Get NASA nighttime lights** -> watch decades of brightness change trace a line a 1930s appraiser drew before WWII -- does the redlined boundary still glow dimmer 90 years later, and where has federal money re-lit the map?
- **Get FEMA NFIP flood claims** -> animate 40 years of federal flood payouts by tract -- who gets rebuilt again and again on the taxpayer's dime, and does the money follow the flood or the votes?
- **Get DoD 1033 police militarization** -> map the flow of MRAPs and rifles from the Pentagon to local departments against police killings -- does the war gear pool in the same counties that were redlined?

## Where power flies (and sails)

- **Get FAA registrations + flight tracks** -> draw the private-jet map of the donor class: match tail numbers to Ripple's biggest donors and contractors, then watch whose plane lands in a district the week before a big check clears or a committee vote flips.
- **Get FHWA National Bridge Inventory** -> map every structurally deficient bridge against the infrastructure money that reached that county and how its member voted -- "voted no on infrastructure, has the worst bridges" is a headline that writes itself.
- **Get FMCSA carrier safety data** -> plot trucking crash and out-of-service rates against federal freight contracts -- do the worst-safety (and chameleon-reincarnated) carriers keep winning the fattest hauling deals, and who do their owners fund?

## The sanctioned world in motion

- **Get US Customs bills of lading** -> put every ocean container a company imports on a map by foreign supplier, then overlay OFAC entities -- a US importer still receiving shipments one hop from a sanctioned party.
- **Get OFAC sanctioned crypto wallets** -> the same SDN entities already in Ripple's vessel graph, extended to their digital money rails -- watch new wallets light up in the days *after* an entity's ship gets flagged. The enforcement cascade.
- **Get US foreign aid + AidData Chinese loans** -> put US aid, US arms, and Chinese Belt-and-Road money into the same country on one map over time -- the great-power bidding war for each nation, checked against how it then votes at the UN.
- **Get OONI internet-censorship measurements** -> animate the blackouts over the world map and watch a spike in blocked messaging apps arrive hours before a protest turns violent. The digital-repression signature.

## The three I'd chase first

1. **Census / ACS tract demographics** -- the single highest-leverage grab: every existing Ripple map becomes per-capita and race-aware in one move, and the 1930s redlining polygons finally answer "who lives there now."
2. **Federal lobbying disclosures (LDA)** -- the missing verb between donation and vote, stitching lobby-spend to the exact bill to the members' actual votes on the money and Congress spines Ripple already owns.
3. **OSHA enforcement** -- easy to get and instantly vivid: a killed-worker dot sitting next to a rising federal-contract line for the same firm, overlaid straight onto the redlining and overdose maps.
