## Final ROI verdict -- the loop stops here
Five rounds, ~300+ datasets banked, and Round 5 kept 49/56 -- but look at *what* it kept: increasingly local (single-state court dumps, one-city TIF ledgers, per-state licensing boards) and increasingly scrape-or-gated rather than clean bulk. The federal, freely-obtainable, nationally-scaled frontier is now effectively mapped -- what remains is either narrow (one state at a time) or expensive to liberate (PDF scrapes, litigation, entity resolution). **The ROI has flipped: from scouting to building.** The corpus these five rounds represent is a near-complete map of the joinable public record -- federal money, courts, health, corporate, environmental, and now the state/local and liberated layers that hang off the same steel keys (FIPS, NPI, EIN, CIK, FEC id, IMO, country ISO). **The loop terminates.** Single highest-leverage next move: stop scouting and start *landing + wiring* the keystones already banked -- Virginia courts, Deportation Data, NY IDA, FracFocus, NYC HPD -- through the five-checkpoint pipeline, because a joined dataset in `LIBRARY_MARTS` is worth ten more sitting in a scouting brief.
